Feynman — Logo Concepts

Five logo concepts built on a core idea: Feynman diagrams as a visual metaphor for the exchange of knowledge between minds, books, and ideas. Each uses the visual language of particle physics — vertices, propagators, fermion lines — to represent conversation and learning.

Design Rationale

Why Feynman diagrams as the logo concept:

  • Direct reference — Richard Feynman literally invented these diagrams. No other visual element is more intrinsically "Feynman."
  • Perfect metaphor — Feynman diagrams depict particle interactions: things meeting, exchanging something, and diverging changed. That's exactly what happens in this product — minds meet, exchange knowledge, and leave enriched.
  • The wavy propagator — the signature wavy line represents a force carrier (photon/boson). In the logo, it becomes the carrier of knowledge and ideas between minds.
  • Vertices as meeting points — in physics, vertices are where interactions happen. In the product, they're where you meet books and great minds.
  • Visual distinctiveness — no other app uses Feynman diagram aesthetics. The wavy-line-with-dots visual language is immediately distinctive and ownable.
  • Scalability — line art with dots works at any size, from favicon (16px) to splash screen.

My recommendation: Concept 3 ("The F Diagram") — it combines brand recognition (the letter F), intellectual gravitas (the Feynman diagram), and visual clarity. It's the only concept that works as both an abstract mark and a readable letter, giving it a natural advantage for branding.
